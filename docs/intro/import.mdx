You can use any of the three following ways to get ValQ added to Microsoft Power BI:

1.  Import from file
2.  Import from AppSource - Marketplace
3.  Import from AppSource - My Organization

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install1.png')} 
    width={430}
    height={395}/>
</div>

## 1. Import from file
This option is used when you have ValQ as a ‘.pbiviz’ file in your local machine.  <br /> <br />
1. a) To get started, click on the three dots under the **Visualizations** pane and choose **Import from file** option
as shown in the image below.
<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install2.png')}
    width={430}
    height={395}/>
</div> <br />

1. b) Now select the **.pbiviz file** available in your local desktop to import ValQ into Power BI. 
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install5.png')} />
</div> <br />

1. c) Once the file gets imported, you will see the message as shown below. Click OK.
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install6.png')} />
</div> <br />

1. d) You have successfully added ValQ to Microsoft Power BI. You will be able to see ValQ now under the **Visualizations** pane. 
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install7.png')} />
</div>

## 2. Import from AppSource - Marketplace
This option is used to download ValQ from the public AppSource marketplace.  <br /> <br />
2. a) To get started, click on the three dots under the **Visualizations** pane and choose **Import from AppSource** option
as shown in the image below.
<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install3.png')}
    width={430}
    height={395}
/>
</div> <br />

2. b) If you have not signed in already, you would be prompted to sign in to access the Marketplace.
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install8.png')} />
</div> <br />

2. c) Simply search for ValQ in the search bar and add the app to Power BI by clicking **Add**.
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install9.png')} />
</div> <br />

2. d) Once the file gets imported, you will see the message as shown below. Click OK.
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install10.png')} />
</div> <br />

2. e) You have successfully added ValQ to Microsoft Power BI. You will be able to see ValQ now under the **Visualizations** pane. 
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install4.png')} />
</div>

## 3. Import from AppSource - My Organization 
This option is used to download ValQ from the public AppSource marketplace. <br /> <br />
3. a) To get started, click on the three dots under the **Visualizations** pane and choose **Import from AppSource** option as shown in the image below.
<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install3.png')}
    width={430}
    height={395}
/>
</div> <br />

3. b) If you have not signed in already, you would be prompted to sign in to access the Marketplace.
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install8.png')} />
</div> <br />

3. c) Go to **My Organization** tab to identify the ValQ app and click **Add** to get ValQ. <br /> Note that ValQ will be available here only if the administrator from your organization has enabled ValQ here.
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install12.png')} />
</div> <br />

3. d) Once the file gets imported, you will see the message as shown below. Click OK.
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install10.png')} />
</div><br />

3. e) You have successfully added ValQ to Microsoft Power BI. You will be able to see ValQ now under the **Visualizations** pane. 
<br />

<div style={{textAlign: 'center'}}>
<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install11.png')} />
</div> <br />

**Using ValQ**:
  - To start using ValQ, click on the ValQ icon. You will see ValQ visual on the canvas.

  <img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install13.png')} />
  - Expand the visual to fit the screen. You can now start using ValQ by assigning data to the visual.
