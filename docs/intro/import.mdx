You can use any of the three following ways to get ValQ added to Microsoft Power BI:
  - Import from file
  - Import from AppSource - Marketplace
  - Import from AppSource - My Organisation

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install1.png')} />

  ## Import from file
  
  Under the first method, let's try to import ValQ when the build is available as a file in your local desktop.

  To get started, click on the three dots option named as Import a custom visual under the Visualizations Pane and 
  choose **Import from file** option as shown in the image below.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install2.png')} />

  Now select the .pbiviz file available in your local desktop to import ValQ into Power BI.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install5.png')} />

  Once the file gets imported, you will see the message as shown below.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install6.png')} />

  ValQ has been successfully added to Microsoft Power BI under the Visualizations pane as seen in the below figure. 

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install7.png')} />

  ## Import from AppSource - Marketplace
  
  Another way to import ValQ is to get the app from **Marketplace** tab. In case you do not have the file, you can always 
  get the app from the appsource.

  To get started, click on the three dots option named as Import a custom visual under the Visualizations Pane
  and choose **Import from AppSource** option as shown in the image below.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install3.png')} />

  Now sign in to your Microsoft Power BI account to access the marketplace.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install8.png')} />

  Simply search for ValQ in the search bar and add the app to Power BI by clicking on Add button.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install9.png')} />

  Once the app gets added, you will see the message as shown below.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install10.png')} />

  ValQ has been successfully added to Microsoft Power BI under the Visualizations pane as seen in the below figure. 

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install4.png')} />

  ## Import from AppSource - My Organisation
  
  The third and final way of importing ValQ is to get the app from **My Organisation** tab found inside the Appsource.This method
  is applied when the admin has enabled ValQ as an app in your business.

  To get started, click on the three dots option named as Import a custom visual under the Visualizations Pane
  and choose **Import from AppSource** option as shown in the image below.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install3.png')} />

  Now sign in to your Microsoft Power BI account to access the marketplace.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install8.png')} />

  Go to My Organization tab to identify the ValQ app and click on add to get ValQ.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install12.png')} />

  Once the app gets added, you will see the message as shown below.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install10.png')} />

  ValQ has been successfully added to Microsoft Power BI under the Visualizations pane as seen in the below figure. 

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install11.png')} />

  The above steps help you to get ValQ on your Power BI Desktop. 
  
  Once ValQ gets added to Power BI, click on the ValQ icon and you will see the screen as the image below. 

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install13.png')} />

  Adjust the size of the tile so the screen appears as the image below and get data to start ValQ.

<img alt="ValQ get" src={useBaseUrl('/doc-images/intro/install14.png')} />
